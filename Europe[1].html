Some of the best foods from across the pond

Europepean Cuisine

From fresh seaside Fish and Chips in England to the savory Banitsas from Bulgaria, Europe has had a lot to offer in the world of food. Across the continent Europe has provided the world with numerous treats that have impacted cultures for generations to come. Intercontinental trade routes like the Columbian Exchange and different colonization efforts led by the Spanish and British resulted in an influx of ingredients that are responsible for modern day European Cuisine. Europe is credited for the ceremonious “5-course meal” construct as well as creating what we know as modern restaurants by the early 1780’s.

Highlighting different foods from Poland, Scotland, Belgium, Italy, and Germany.

Top Foods from Europe
    POLISH PIEROGI

    Pierogi are pan-fried dumplings from the eastern European nation of Poland. Pierogi are usually filled with savory or sweet fillings like ground beef or sausage, potatoes, cheese, and even fruit. Pierogi is often served with different dipping sauces depending on its contents, sour cream mixed with sugar for sweet or bacon fat for savory. Pierogies are meaningful and are served at almost every holiday as well as mournings or wakes as a symbol of comfort and togetherness.

    SCOTTISH HAGGIS

    Haggis is a pudding from Scotland that can be traced back to 1430. Haggis consist of sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs that is mixed with onion, oatmeal, and other spices stuffed in an animal’s stomach. Haggis became one of the most crucial meals during the economic crash in the late 17th century because of it’s inexpensive ingredients. This economic depression led to a seven year famine in Scotland, leaving many families to look towards dishes like Haggis due to its heartiness and affordability.

    Belgian Waffles

    Created in 1958, Belgian Waffles quickly became a staple for breakfast throughout several parts of Europe. These unique waffles contain deeper pockets compared to waffles from across the pond designed to hold sweet pockets of syrup. These decadent waffles are typically stacked high with freshly cut strawberries, fluffy whipped cream, covered in powdered sugar; other toppings include chocolate chips, blueberries, chocolate sauce, or even nutella. Belgian Waffles are also served as dessert when topped with ice cream.

    Italian Pizza

    The origins of this delicious flatbread can be traced back to the battlefield during the Roman Empire known then as focaccia and the term pizza didn't come about until 997 A.D. in the Italian city of Gaeta. The savory sauced covered pizza as we know and love today didn't surface until the end of the 18th century in Naples, Italy. American troops were introduced to pizza when stationed in Italy during WWII, but it wasn't introduced to the mainland until the 19th century when Italian immigrants arrived in the United States.

    German Bratwurst

    Bratwurst is a delicacy that has been enjoyed for generations throughout German history. Dating back to as far as 228 AD this savory sausage has become one of the most popular snacks throughout parts of western Europe. Bratwurst is a sausage that normally is made of pork, but can also contain beef or veal. There are over 40 different variations of German Bratwurst each differing by the content inside of the casing or by even how the sausage is served, the type being served depends on where you are located in the country.
